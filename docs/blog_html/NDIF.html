Example Press Release: NDIF Opens Its Doors to Technical Applicants

Work with National Deep Inference Fabric, apply today

The National Deep Inference Fabric has announced a new enginnering summer fellowship for which applicants can begin applying immediately until 2/7/2025 to participate in the high-priority development and scaling of AI infrastructure. With a generous NSF grant project, NFID speeds forwards to develop "an innovative, highly-transparent large-scale AI inference infrastructure to enable scientific research on the largest open AI models," and they need the aid of committed systems and ML students.

This initiative is part of the NDIF mission of bringing interpretable neural networks to the masses, with the funding and interest of the public.

NDIF is an @NSF-supported computational infrastructure project to help YOU advance the science of large-scale AI," tweeted David Bau, director and PI of NDIF. There work is to create "Transparent, democratic access for AI that will scale as models grow" (David Bau).

Working fellows will be supervised by the NDIF team of the leading machine learning and software engineering systems professors in-person at Bostom from May 18th to August 2nd, with NDIF-funded housing accomodations if requested. The fellowship is working at a running pace with the ever developing field of AI, and needs fellows who can match and keep the pace. There work is essential, and NEIF will make sure that the selected candiddates are able to make the schedule!

the NDIF team is looking for a Full Stack development fellow, Site Reliability engineering fellow, and an Outreach fellow. For more details, visit ndif.us/fellowship.html.

Link: https://ndif.us/fellowship.html. Resource: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5e/Wikimedia_Foundation_press_release_template.pdf
